The original. The IBM Personal Computer Model 5150 — the machine that launched a thousand clones and defined what a "PC" means to this day. Released August 12, 1981, it wasn't the most powerful micro on the market, but it had three letters on the front that made corporate America pay attention: IBM.

Open architecture meant third parties could build expansion cards and eventually entire compatible systems. The rest is history.
